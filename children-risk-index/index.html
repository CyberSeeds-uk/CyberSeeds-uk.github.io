Children’s Digital Risk Index

A simple guide to understanding your child's online risk level.

How This Index Works

Children’s digital risk depends on age, access, experience, maturity and exposure. This simple, parent-friendly index gives an approximate understanding of where a child may sit.

Note: Every child is unique. This guide is designed to help parents reflect on digital habits and take practical steps toward safer use.

Risk Levels by Age Group

Ages 3–7: Low → Medium

  • ✔ Limited awareness of online risks
  • ✔ High curiosity; low caution
  • ✔ Risk increases with unsupervised tablet/YouTube use
  • ✔ Strong parent guidance recommended

Ages 8–11: Medium → High

  • ✔ First exposure to chats, games & social features
  • ✔ More independent device use
  • ✔ Vulnerable to scams, fake messaging & grooming attempts
  • ✔ Privacy settings & safe search needed

Ages 12–15: High Risk

  • ✔ Social media, messaging & gaming unsupervised
  • ✔ Cyberbullying, grooming & exposure to harmful content
  • ✔ Peer pressure & identity risks increase
  • ✔ Strong boundaries & digital routine essential

Ages 16–18: High → Severe

  • ✔ Full social platform access
  • ✔ Online financial scams rise sharply
  • ✔ Exposure to adult content & communities
  • ✔ Digital independence but weak protection

How to Reduce Digital Risk

  • ✔ Set up strong device & app privacy settings
  • ✔ Use parental controls where needed
  • ✔ Build a predictable digital routine
  • ✔ Talk openly about online behaviour
  • ✔ Reduce unsupervised screen time for younger children
  • ✔ Keep gaming chats and friend lists monitored
  • ✔ Learn how scams target young users

The Cyber Seeds 5-Point Audit includes screening and parental guidance for all age groups.
