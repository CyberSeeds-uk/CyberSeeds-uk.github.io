Phishing & Scam Protection

Spot fake links, dangerous messages and online fraud attempts

What Is Phishing?

Phishing is when scammers try to trick you into giving away personal details, passwords, bank information or control of your accounts.

They often pretend to be:

  • ✔ Banks, delivery companies, HMRC
  • ✔ PayPal, Amazon, Apple, Netflix
  • ✔ Schools or gaming platforms
  • ✔ “Support teams” offering help

Common Scam Signs

  • ✔ Urgent messages (“Your account will be closed!”)
  • ✔ Unknown numbers texting delivery updates
  • ✔ Emails asking you to “confirm your login”
  • ✔ Fake links (with misspellings or odd endings)
  • ✔ Unexpected password reset emails
  • ✔ Someone claiming your child has done something online

If a message makes you panic, it’s almost always a scam.

How to Stay Safe

  • ✔ Never click links in texts/emails you weren’t expecting
  • ✔ Type the website address manually
  • ✔ Don’t respond to unknown numbers
  • ✔ Don’t share codes sent to your phone
  • ✔ Use 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)
  • ✔ Keep banking details private
  • ✔ Teach children not to trust messages from strangers

If You Think You’ve Been Scammed

  • ✔ Change your passwords immediately
  • ✔ Check for unknown devices logged in
  • ✔ Enable 2FA on important accounts
  • ✔ Contact your bank if money was involved
  • ✔ Secure your email (often the main target)
