Online Safety Basics

Online safety isn’t about fear — it’s about awareness and simple habits that protect your home, your data and your children.

Why Online Safety Matters

Every day we use apps, accounts, social media and online services without thinking about how much information we’re giving away — or who can see it.

Cybercriminals rely on small mistakes, quick actions and tiny gaps in awareness.

What You Should Know:
  • Most online attacks don’t feel like attacks — they look like normal messages, links or login pages.
  • Small bits of personal information can be combined to guess passwords or access accounts.
  • Children and adults are both targeted because they act quickly and trust interfaces.
  • Social profiles reveal much more than we realise — locations, routines, relationships and habits.
  • Many breaches start with a single careless tap.

Essential Online Safety Steps

1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Never reuse passwords. If one account is hacked, every other account becomes vulnerable.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

This single step blocks most unauthorised access attempts.

3. Keep Social Media Private

Limit your posts, your friends list, and who can message or tag you.

4. Think Before You Click

If a link feels urgent, emotional or unexpected — stop. It’s likely a scam or phishing attempt.

5. Review App Permissions

Many apps request access to your microphone, camera, photos or location unnecessarily. Remove what you don’t trust.

6. Be Careful With Public Wi-Fi

Avoid logging into bank accounts or private services when on public hotspots.

7. Teach Children “Pause Before You Post”

One quick post can reveal school names, locations, friendships, routines and schedule details.
