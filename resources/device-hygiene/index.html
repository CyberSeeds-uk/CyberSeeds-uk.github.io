Device Hygiene

Outdated devices and apps are the easiest entry point for attackers. Small hygiene habits protect your entire digital home.

Why Device Hygiene Matters

Every device you use — smartphones, tablets, laptops, smart TVs, consoles and even smart toys — holds sensitive information and connects to your home network.

When devices fall behind on updates or use insecure settings, they become **easy targets** for attackers, malware, fake apps and data theft.

What You Should Know:
  • Old apps often keep sensitive data, even long after you stop using them.
  • Devices without updates can be exploited in seconds by automated attacks.
  • Fake apps and unsafe downloads are designed to trick both adults and children.
  • Weak device security can expose Wi-Fi passwords, locations, photos and messages.

How to Protect Your Devices

1. Turn On Automatic Updates

Updates fix vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates for apps and system software on all devices.

2. Delete Apps You Don’t Use

Old apps can leak data or contain outdated code. Remove anything you no longer recognise or need.

3. Avoid Third-Party Downloads

Only install apps through the official Apple App Store, Google Play Store or the device's trusted marketplace.

4. Enable Device Lock & Biometrics

Use Face ID, fingerprint unlock or a strong PIN. Avoid using “1234” or simple patterns.

5. Restart Your Devices Weekly

A quick restart clears temporary exploits, improves performance and adds protection.

6. Remove Old Devices from Your Wi-Fi

Devices you no longer use — old tablets, old phones, forgotten smart plugs — still pose a risk. Remove them from your Wi-Fi and reset them.
