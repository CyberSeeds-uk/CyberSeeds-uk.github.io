Children’s Digital Risk Index

Children explore the online world long before parents see the hidden risks. This guide helps you understand the dangers and build a safer digital home.

Why This Matters

Children spend time on platforms designed with complexity, hidden features and social mechanics that even adults don’t fully understand.

Games like Roblox, Fortnite, Minecraft and social apps like TikTok give children access to millions of strangers — often without realising it.

What You Should Know:
  • Children often talk to strangers through in-game chat, voice chat and friend requests.
  • Games contain private servers, hidden chats and unmoderated spaces.
  • Scammers target children because they respond quickly and trust easily.
  • Children rarely report worrying interactions because they fear losing their devices or privileges.
  • Many parents never see the chats, invites or server history children receive.

Common Hidden Risks

1. In-Game Chat

Many games allow strangers to message children directly. Even kid-friendly platforms include public, private and group chats.

2. Friend Requests from Unknown Players

Children accept friend requests quickly, often thinking “it’s just a game” — not realising strangers can message them privately.

3. Private Servers

Some Roblox, Minecraft and Discord communities run private servers with no moderation. Children can be exposed to inappropriate behaviour or scary content.

4. Scams, Robux Theft & Phishing

Children are often lured by free currency, items or skins — leading to account loss, data exposure or unwanted conversations with older users.

5. Pressure, Bullying & Manipulation

Children may feel pressured to join certain games, share personal information or send voice notes to “fit in”.

How to Reduce the Risks

1. Turn On Privacy & Chat Restrictions

Most platforms include strong parental controls. Restrict messages to “Friends Only” or disable them completely.

2. Review Friends Lists Weekly

Help your child understand who is a friend and who is not. Remove unknown or suspicious accounts.

3. Keep Devices in Shared Family Areas

Children are less likely to hide worrying interactions when they feel supported rather than monitored.

4. Talk Openly About Online Safety

Instead of punishment, focus on curiosity: “If anything online makes you uncomfortable, tell me — you won't get in trouble.”

5. Avoid Allowing Children on Adult Platforms

Discord, Snapchat, and some Roblox servers are designed for older audiences. Review the minimum age recommendations.
