Wi-Fi Safety

Your router is the digital front door to your home. If it’s weak, outdated or misconfigured, anyone nearby can attempt to get in.

Why Wi-Fi Security Matters

Every device in your home connects through your router — phones, tablets, consoles, TVs, smart speakers, cameras and more. If someone gains access to your Wi-Fi, they can see or target everything connected to it.

What You Should Know:
  • Many routers still use weak default passwords.
  • Old routers often contain unpatched vulnerabilities.
  • Anyone within range — even neighbours — can attempt to access unsecured Wi-Fi.
  • Unprotected Wi-Fi can expose devices, chats, files and private information.

How to Protect Your Wi-Fi

1. Change Your Router Password

Your router password should be long, unique and not shared with anyone outside your household.

2. Update Your Router Firmware

Most people never update their router. Firmware updates fix serious security flaws — check your provider’s app or login page.

3. Turn Off WPS (Wi-Fi Protected Setup)

WPS is a known vulnerability. If your router allows it, disable WPS for stronger security.

4. Use WPA2 or WPA3 Encryption

Older security modes like WEP are extremely weak. Set your router to WPA2 or WPA3 only.

5. Separate Your Guest Network

If visitors connect to your Wi-Fi, use a guest network. This protects your main devices from exposure.

6. Reboot Your Router Monthly

Restarting your router helps clear temporary vulnerabilities and keeps performance stable.
