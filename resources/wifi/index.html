WiFi Safety Guide

Simple steps to secure your home router & WiFi network.

1. Change Your Default Router Password

Most routers ship with weak default admin passwords. Log in and set a strong, unique password immediately.

2. Update Your Router Firmware

Keeping your router updated protects against known vulnerabilities used by attackers.

3. Use WPA3 or WPA2 Encryption

Open networks or outdated protocols can expose your family to attackers nearby.

4. Hide or Rename Your Network

Use a neutral network name — avoid personal information like family names or house numbers.

5. Turn Off WPS

WPS is convenient but insecure. Disable it in settings to prevent brute-force attacks.

6. Separate Guest WiFi

Keep guests away from your main network. Use a dedicated guest network.
