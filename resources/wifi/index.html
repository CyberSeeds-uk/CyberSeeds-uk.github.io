Wi-Fi Safety

Your router is the digital front door to your home. If it’s weak or outdated, anyone nearby can attempt to get in.

Why Wi-Fi Security Matters

Every device in your home connects through your router — phones, tablets, consoles, TVs, smart speakers, cameras, doorbells and more.

If someone gains access to your Wi-Fi, they can see or target everything connected to it.

What You Should Know

  • Many routers still use weak or default passwords.
  • Old routers often contain unpatched vulnerabilities.
  • Anyone within range — even neighbours — can attempt to access unsecured Wi-Fi.
  • Unprotected Wi-Fi can expose devices, chats, photos, files and private information.

How to Protect Your Wi-Fi

1. Change Your Router Password

Your router password should be long, unique and not shared with anyone outside your household. This prevents strangers from logging into your router’s control panel.

2. Update Your Router Firmware

Most people never update their router. Firmware updates fix critical security flaws — check your provider’s app or login page.

3. Use WPA3 or WPA2 Security

These encryption standards protect your Wi-Fi from being intercepted. If your router is using WEP or Open, it must be changed immediately.

4. Hide or Rename Your Wi-Fi Network

Avoid using names like “SmithFamilyHome”. Use a neutral name so strangers cannot identify your household.

5. Turn Off WPS

WPS (the quick connect button) is often exploited by attackers. Turning it off significantly strengthens your Wi-Fi.

6. Create a Guest Network

This keeps visitors off your main devices and prevents accidental exposure from infected phones or laptops.

7. Reboot Your Router Weekly

A simple restart clears old sessions and can disconnect unwanted devices.
