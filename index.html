Why “signal”, not “score”

Scores often trigger shame or perfectionism. A signal does something better: it shows where risk flows in the household system — and the smallest move that changes it.

System, not blame

We map causes, not “fault”.

Golden nugget: Most household risk isn’t “bad behaviour” — it’s friction: too many accounts, too many settings, and no simple ritual to keep it stable.

  • Reduce friction first: pick one weekly “reset” habit (10 minutes) instead of chasing perfect settings.
  • Move risk away from tiredness: do changes at calm times, not during arguments or late nights.

Proportionate actions

The smallest change that matters most.

Golden nugget: The best household security upgrades are one-step multipliers — they protect many things at once.

  • Password manager + 2-step protects email, banking, socials, school apps in one move.
  • Guest Wi-Fi isolates risky devices without needing “tech skills”.

Trauma-aware by default

Language that keeps people safe to learn.

Golden nugget: When people feel judged, they hide problems — and hidden problems become incidents.

  • Use “signals”: “We’re seeing risk flow here” instead of “you should have…”.
  • Start with relief: “Nothing leaves your device” reduces fear and increases honesty.

The Five Lenses

Tap a lens to open deeper insight — no new page, no jargon.

Network

Wi-Fi, router, device separation

Hidden risk most homes miss: the Wi-Fi password can be strong, but the router admin login stays default — that’s where takeovers happen.

  • Seed: change router admin password + turn off WPS.
  • Seed: enable guest Wi-Fi for visitors + smart devices.

Devices

Updates, locks, backups, apps

Hidden risk: one old device becomes the “soft door” into accounts via saved sessions and notifications.

  • Seed: auto-updates ON + 6-digit lock + biometrics.
  • Seed: one monthly “app permission sweep”.

Privacy

Accounts, exposure, identity

Hidden risk: “recovery paths” (email/SMS) are often weaker than passwords — attackers target recovery.

  • Seed: protect email first (2-step + recovery review).
  • Seed: remove phone number from public socials.

Scams

Fraud, phishing, pressure tactics

Hidden risk: scams succeed through urgency. A household “pause protocol” beats any antivirus.

  • Seed: “two-channel check” for money requests.
  • Seed: one family “safe phrase” for payments.

Wellbeing

Boundaries, sleep, conflict

Hidden risk: sleep loss makes every other lens weaker — judgement, impulse control, scam resistance all drop.

  • Seed: one charging “dock” outside bedrooms.
  • Seed: notifications OFF for the apps that cause arguments.

Principles

A household method: calm, repeatable, real.

Map before you mend

See the system, then change it.

Most fixes fail because people patch symptoms. Cyber Seeds starts with a map: devices, accounts, routines, stress points.

  • Seed: write the “top 5 accounts that matter” (email, banking, Apple/Google, school, primary social).

Small moves win

Tiny actions that protect many things.

We prioritise multipliers: changes that cut risk across the whole home.

  • Seed: 2-step on email + password manager = whole-house uplift.

Dignity by design

No shame language, ever.

Safety improves fastest when people can admit what’s real. We design for honesty: calm wording, no judgement, no surveillance.

  • Seed: replace “who’s to blame?” with “what’s the next calm step?”
